Digital Xrays

As one of the oldest and most common forms of medical imaging, X-rays are called radiography, or x-rays. The X-ray machine produces a controlled beam of radiation, which is used to create an image of the inside of your body by creating a controlled beam of radiation. This beam is directed at the area being examined.

Colour Doppler

This technique estimates the average velocity of flow within a vessel by color coding the information. The direction of blood flow is assigned the color red or blue, indicating flow toward or away from the ultrasound transducer.

Ultrasound

A medical ultrasound (also known as diagnostic sonography or ultrasonography) is a diagnostic imaging technique based on the use of ultrasound waves to diagnose medical conditions. As a result, it is used to examine internal body structures such as tendons, muscles, joints, blood vessels, and internal organs that are located inside the body.

ECG

A recording of the electrical activity of the heart. Abbreviated ECG and EKG. ... Electrodes are placed on the skin of the chest and connected in a specific order to a machine that, when turned on, measures electrical activity all over the heart.
